# Log Analysis with Splunk or Alternative Tools

## Objective

The objective of this lab is to practice reviewing log data to identify patterns, suspicious behaviour, and useful investigation points relevant to SOC and security analyst workflows.

## Scenario

In this lab, I worked with log data to understand how analysts can review events, identify anomalies, and build context during monitoring and triage. The focus was on structured analysis rather than tool-specific expertise alone.

## Key Areas Reviewed

- Event patterns across log records
- Repeated or unusual activity
- Authentication-related events
- Source and destination observations
- Time-based review of suspicious behaviour
- Indicators that may support escalation or further investigation

## What I Practiced

- Reading and interpreting log data
- Looking for suspicious or unusual activity patterns
- Thinking in a SIEM-style investigation workflow
- Recording observations clearly and logically
- Building analyst-focused documentation

## Outcome

This lab helped strengthen my understanding of how log analysis supports security monitoring, triage, and investigation work in real-world SOC environments.

## Skills Demonstrated

- Log analysis
- SIEM-style thinking
- Event correlation awareness
- Investigation structure
- Security operations mindset
