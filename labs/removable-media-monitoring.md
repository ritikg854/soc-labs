# Removable Media Monitoring Lab

## Objective

The objective of this lab is to understand how removable media activity can create security risk in enterprise environments and how monitoring such activity supports security operations and data protection efforts.

## Scenario

In this lab, I explored the security relevance of removable media usage such as USB devices and external storage. The focus was on understanding why this activity matters, what types of risks may be involved, and how analysts may approach review or escalation.

## Key Areas Reviewed

- Use of USB and external storage devices
- Data transfer risk
- Policy and control awareness
- Endpoint visibility considerations
- User activity context
- Indicators that may require review or escalation

## What I Practiced

- Thinking about removable media risk from a security operations perspective
- Understanding how endpoint and DLP-related concerns may overlap
- Identifying situations where monitoring may be important
- Writing structured notes that reflect analyst-style thinking
- Connecting practical enterprise risk to security workflows

## Outcome

This lab helped strengthen my understanding of endpoint-related security monitoring and the role of removable media awareness in supporting data protection and operational security.
