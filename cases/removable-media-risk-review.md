# Removable Media Risk Review

## Objective

The purpose of this case study is to practice documenting the security relevance of removable media activity and assess when such behaviour may require monitoring, review, or escalation.

## Scenario

In this exercise, I reviewed a scenario involving removable media usage in an enterprise environment. The goal was to assess why the activity might matter from a security and data protection perspective, and how an analyst might document the situation.

## Investigation Focus

- USB or external storage usage
- Context of the user activity
- Potential data handling concerns
- Endpoint visibility and control considerations
- Whether activity appears routine, unauthorised, or higher risk

## Analyst Approach

- Consider what type of removable media activity occurred
- Review whether the behaviour appears expected or unusual
- Think about data protection and policy-related implications
- Assess whether monitoring, restriction, or escalation may be appropriate
- Document findings clearly in a structured way

## Key Learning

This case study helped reinforce the importance of endpoint visibility, removable media awareness, and practical risk assessment in enterprise security operations.

## Skills Demonstrated

- Endpoint risk awareness
- DLP-related reasoning
- Security documentation
- Analyst-style review
- Enterprise security thinking
