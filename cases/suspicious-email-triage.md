# Suspicious Email Triage

## Objective

The purpose of this case study is to practice initial triage of a suspicious email and document the reasoning used to assess whether the message may represent a phishing attempt.

## Scenario

In this exercise, I reviewed a suspicious email that showed characteristics commonly associated with phishing. The goal was to analyze the visible indicators, assess risk, and structure the findings in a clear analyst-style format.

## Investigation Focus

- Sender legitimacy
- Domain review
- Urgency or pressure tactics
- Suspicious links or misleading text
- Potential impersonation
- Attachment or credential theft risk

## Analyst Approach

- Review the sender identity and domain carefully
- Assess whether the subject and message create urgency or pressure
- Check for suspicious wording, unusual requests, or impersonation signals
- Examine whether links or attachments could present risk
- Document observations and determine whether escalation would be appropriate

## Key Learning

This case study helped strengthen practical phishing triage thinking and improved my ability to document suspicious email analysis in a clear and structured way.

## Analyst Takeaway

This case study helped reinforce the importance of structured triage, context-based review, and clear documentation in security operations work.

## Skills Demonstrated

- Phishing awareness
- Initial triage
- Investigation structure
- Risk-oriented thinking
- Security documentation
