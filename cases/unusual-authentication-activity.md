# Unusual Authentication Activity

## Objective

The purpose of this case study is to practice reviewing authentication-related log activity and document how unusual patterns may be assessed during security monitoring and triage.

## Scenario

In this exercise, I reviewed authentication-focused log activity to identify signs that may require further investigation. The goal was to build a structured approach to recognising unusual access behaviour, repeated attempts, or account-related anomalies.

## Investigation Focus

- Repeated authentication events
- Unusual timing or frequency
- Account-related anomalies
- Possible failed and successful login patterns
- Activity that may justify escalation or additional review

## Analyst Approach

- Review authentication events in context
- Look for repeated or unusual behaviour over time
- Consider whether the activity may be expected, misconfigured, or suspicious
- Note the importance of user, host, and timing context
- Document findings in a structured triage format

## Key Learning

This case study helped strengthen my understanding of authentication monitoring and improved my ability to document suspicious access patterns in an analyst-oriented way.

## Analyst Takeaway

This case study helped reinforce the importance of structured triage, context-based review, and clear documentation in security operations work.

## Skills Demonstrated

- Authentication log review
- Security monitoring awareness
- Triage reasoning
- Investigation documentation
- Analyst-focused thinking
